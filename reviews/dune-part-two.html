The Leftovers

2014–2017 • HBO • 3 Seasons

What happens when 2% of the world's population vanishes without explanation? This series, based on Tom Perrotta's novel, explores grief, faith, and meaning with unflinching honesty. Damon Lindelof's adaptation quickly transcends its source material to become something extraordinary.

Season 1 sets up the mystery and raw trauma. Season 2 in Australia is arguably some of the best television ever made—a seismic shift in setting that deepens every character. Season 3 provides a haunting, ambiguous conclusion that rewards patient viewers.

The performances are uniformly excellent, especially Justin Theroux, Carrie Coon, and Christopher Eccleston. The score by Max Richter is transcendent. This is a show about living with the unanswerable, and it finds beauty in the struggle.
