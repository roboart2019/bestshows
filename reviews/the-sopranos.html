The Sopranos

1999–2007 · Max · 6 Seasons

The Sopranos invented the blueprint for everything that followed. David Chase's portrait of New Jersey mob boss Tony Soprano — in therapy, managing his crew, struggling with his family — established what prestige drama could be: morally complex, cinematic, and unafraid of ambiguity.

James Gandolfini's performance is one of the finest in American acting. Tony is violent, funny, perceptive, and pathetic — a man who can identify exactly why he is unhappy but is constitutionally incapable of changing. The therapy sessions with Dr. Melfi (Lorraine Bracco) are some of the sharpest scenes in television history.

The show's refusal to provide conventional payoff — the infamous cut to black finale — remains the most debated ending in TV history. On rewatch, it's the only possible conclusion for a show about a man who cannot escape himself. Nothing has fully matched it since.
