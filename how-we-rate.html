How We Rate

Our scoring system, explained plainly — no hidden agenda, no payola.

The Overall Score

Every show and film receives a single critic score out of 10. This is not an average of the four breakdown dimensions — it's a holistic editorial judgment that weights all factors, including cultural impact, originality, and how well a work achieves its own goals.

A 9.0 or above is exceptional — reserved for works we consider essential. A 7.5–8.9 is excellent. Below 7.5 means it's good but flawed. We don't review things we consider mediocre or bad; there are enough bad review sites already.

The Four Critic Dimensions

Each title is also scored on four specific dimensions, displayed as rating bars on every review page. These give a more nuanced picture than a single number.

Story & Writing

Narrative structure, pacing, dialogue, thematic depth, and the quality of the screenplay or writers' room. Does it earn its runtime? Does the story go somewhere meaningful?

Performances

The quality of the acting, casting, and character development. This includes ensemble work — a great cast where everyone earns their scenes scores higher than a single standout surrounded by weakness.

Direction & Craft

Visual storytelling, cinematography, editing, score, production design, and sound. The craft score rewards shows and films where the technical elements actively serve the story rather than just looking expensive.

Rewatchability

How well it holds up the second, third, or tenth time. Some shows reveal new layers on rewatch; others burn bright once and fade. This isn't a flaw either way — it's useful information for how you might engage with it.

Official Ratings

We display the official content rating assigned by the MPAA (for films) or the TV Parental Guidelines (for television). These are not our ratings — they're the industry-standard classifications:

  • TV-Y / TV-Y7 — Children's programming
  • TV-G / G — General audiences, all ages
  • TV-PG / PG — Parental guidance suggested
  • TV-14 / PG-13 — Parents strongly cautioned, may be unsuitable for under-14/13
  • TV-MA / R — Mature audiences; not suitable for under 17
  • NC-17 — Adults only

    Member Ratings

    Registered members can rate any show or film on a 1–10 scale. Member ratings are independent of the critic score. The community average is displayed on each review page alongside the number of ratings received.

    Member ratings are anonymous to other users — only you can see your personal score. The community average is the only aggregated number we show.

    Members can also add titles to their Watchlist — a personal list accessible from the members dashboard to track what you want to watch next.

    What We Don't Do

    • We don't accept payment for reviews or placement.
    • We don't adjust scores based on studio relationships.
    • We don't review things we consider below our threshold — we'd rather say nothing than inflate a mediocre show.
    • We don't aggregate scores from other critics. Everything here is original editorial opinion.
